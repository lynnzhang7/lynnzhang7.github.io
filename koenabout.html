Hello there! My name is Koen Yi and I am corporate lawyer in Toronto with a passion for visual arts. My artistic endeavours are predominately impressionist. I use a variety of media ranging from charcoal to liquid white-out to acrylic paints.

My long-term goal is to become a mixed-media impressionist artist.

Why mixed-media?
A few years back, I started challenging the convention that a composition should consist of a single medium. Why pigeonhole yourself by exclusively using one type of paint? Watercolour is light and transparent. Oils are heavy and opaque. In a given image, there are parts that demand transparency and others that demand layers. In a landscape painting, I would use watercolour to push objects in the background further into the distance and then oils to bring attention to the foreground. In this way, further depth can be manufactured by bringing together the unique offerings of each type of paint. I have experimented on the grayscale by using a mix of pencil smudging, liquid white-up and charcoal.
Why impressionism?
My philosophy of art has always been centrist. If we consider abstract expressionism one end of the spectrum and pure academia the other, I fall somewhere in the middle. In other words, while I believe that technical aestheticism is an inherent metric of art, I also believe that the artist’s function is not that of a camera. A great artist expresses in an image something unobservable in objective reality—the subject perspective of the artist himself. Impressionism embodies this philosophy perfectly: it is not completely divorced from objective reality as the subject matter is of objective reality, but it is imputed with the distinctive stylistic elements that depict the artist’s state of mind.

I have been trained in academic sketching for several years prior to graduating secondary school in 2009. In grade 8, I enrolled in the Byng Arts Mini School, a program dedicated to development of the fine arts. I won the award for visual arts for my year in grade 9, and then the same award in the International Baccalaureate program when I transferred in grade 10. During this time, I also participated in several national visual art contests, such as the Royal Canadian Legion Art contest. I am currently a hobbyist and enjoy painting in my spare time.
