The Project

Holographic Cell Visualizer is a mixed reality HoloLens application for cancer researchers, developed by interns from the Microsoft Garage program. Our application enables researchers to intuitively interact with and explore holograms of 3D genomic data.

Microsoft Garage

The Microsoft Garage program is a 16 week internship, where interns are typically put in groups of six — five developers, one designer — to focus on a single project. Most of these projects start from scratch, but my team had the unique opportunity to carry on a project that was started by a previous intern group.

The project that we started on already had the major components of our app down: the processing and rendering of the 50,000 cells, different modes to explore different aspects of the data, and the beginnings of a companion web application. When my intern group started, we reassessed the state of the project with our sponsor BC Cancer, and decided on the three key features we wanted to add.

Intuitive Interactions

One of the major benefits of HoloLens over other 3D imaging platforms is how intuitive it is to interact with. To take advantage of this, we wanted to add the ability to use HoloLens gestures to move the cell model.

Sticky Note Integration

Besides BC Cancer, our other sponsor was Microsoft's Sticky Notes team. Our goal was the integrate the Sticky Notes API into our app so that researchers could place holographic notes and access them from anywhere.

Jupyter Notebook API

Our most important goal was to find a way to integrate our application into a cancer researcher's workflow. We decided to focus on Jupyter Notebook since it is one of the most widely used tools for data analysis.
